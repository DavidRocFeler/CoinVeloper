Tether teams up with Chainalysis to track USDT usage in secondary markets

Strengthening Oversight: Tether Partners with Chainalysis to Monitor USDT in Secondary Markets

Tether announces partnership with Chainalysis Aims to enhance monitoring of USDT in secondary markets New tools will be utilized to prevent USDT from being used for illicit activities Comes amid increased regulatory scrutiny on USDT and Tether The announcement was made in a statement released by the Tether team on their official blog, stating that this partnership will enable better oversight of secondary markets linked to USDT, beyond the entities directly buying and selling the stablecoin to the issuing company. To achieve this, they will leverage a solution provided by Chainalysis, allowing for more precise tracking of transactions. Regarding this, the statement reads:

"Esta solución diseñada por Chainalysis permite a Tether monitorear metódicamente las transacciones, ofreciendo una mejor comprensión y supervisión del mercado de USDT. También servirá como una fuente proactiva de inteligencia para los investigadores y profesionales de cumplimiento de Tether, permitiéndoles identificar billeteras que pueden presentar riesgos o pueden estar asociadas con direcciones ilícitas y/o sancionadas."

Sanctions Monitoring: Matching against a list of addresses and transactions involving sanctioned entities. Categorization Mechanism: Detailed breakdown of USDT holders by type, highlighting exchanges and markets operating in the Dark Web. Wallet Analysis: Service for in-depth examination of the movements of the most significant USDT holders. Illicit Transfer Detector: Identifying operations potentially associated with criminal activities.

Regulatory Spotlight: Tether and Chainalysis Collaboration Amidst Increased Regulatory Scrutiny

The announcement of the partnership between Tether and Chainalysis comes at a time of heightened regulatory focus within the cryptocurrency industry, particularly from the United States government, which has been taking enforcement actions against projects and services through various agencies.

The UN recently identified the USDT stablecoin as one of the mechanisms for money laundering in certain regions of Asia. In response, Tether referenced their ongoing efforts to combat the illicit use of the currency, emphasizing the transparency inherent in these financial technologies.

The company regularly reports on the blocking of addresses associated with sanctions or criminal activities, demonstrating their willingness to collaborate closely with authorities to support investigations. For instance, amid reports suggesting Venezuela's potential use of USDT for oil trading, Tether affirmed its commitment to take action against any addresses found violating OFAC directives.

This partnership between Tether and Chainalysis aims to enhance monitoring capabilities in secondary markets linked to USDT beyond direct transactions with the issuing company. By leveraging Chainalysis' tools, they seek to improve oversight and prevent USDT from being used for illicit activities, contributing to a safer and more compliant cryptocurrency ecosystem.
