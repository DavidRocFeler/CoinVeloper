Cardano whales accumulate this massive daily figure despite a 30% drop: Is a bullish rebound on the horizon?

ADA Whale Activity Sparks Speculation: Is a Short-Term Rebound in Sight?

Cardano whales continue to accumulate altcoins despite a 30% drop in its value over the past month. A blockchain data provider indicated in a post on X that "over the last week, ADA has seen an average daily of $13.84 billion in large transactions."

According to the user, this represents a third of Bitcoin's (BTC) current volume, five times more than Litecoin (LTC) volume, and over 16 times that of the leading meme coin Dogecoin (DOGE). On the other hand, data observed that the coin witnessed an increase in its daily count of whale transactions exceeding $100,000 this Monday, April 29, when these transactions totaled 1,776, the highest daily count for the coin since November 8, 2023.

According to the data provider, "these peaks in whale activity historically correlate with potential price reversals." But now the question is, is ADA poised for a short-term rebound?

Renewed Activity Sparks Speculation

An assessment of its performance on a 1-day chart showed that the coin's price positioned below its 20 and 50-day moving averages (MA). These MAs typically serve as support levels. Therefore, when an asset's price falls to these levels, it usually suggests a temporary pause in the price decline as buyers step in.

However, if the price falls below these MAs with strong selling volume, it could confirm the bearish trend signal. ADA's price trended below these MAs on April 1. The decrease in its Relative Strength Index (RSI) indicator confirmed the subsequent increase in selling pressure on the coin since then.

ADA's RSI is 35.67 at the time of this publication, suggesting that the coin would soon be oversold if the selling momentum persisted. Since its price fell below the MAs on April 1, ADA's price dropped by double digits, as observed by CoinMarketCap.
