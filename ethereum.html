WHAT IS ETHEREUM?

Ethereum is a decentralized blockchain platform and cryptocurrency that enables the creation and execution of smart contracts and decentralized applications (DApps). It was proposed in late 2013 by Vitalik Buterin, a programmer and cryptocurrency researcher, and went live on July 30, 2015.

BEGINNING OF THE ETHEREUM (ETH)

Vitalik Buterin is the creator of Ethereum. He envisioned Ethereum as a platform that would enable developers to create decentralized applications and smart contracts, providing them with greater flexibility and functionality compared to existing blockchain platforms like Bitcoin. Ethereum was born out of the desire to extend the capabilities of blockchain beyond simple peer-to-peer transactions, allowing developers to build decentralized applications and smart contracts. These smart contracts are self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement directly written into code. They automatically enforce and execute the terms of the agreement when predefined conditions are met, eliminating the need for intermediaries in many contractual agreements.

BACKING TECHNOLOGY

The technology that underpins Ethereum is similar to that of Bitcoin, utilizing a decentralized network of nodes to validate and record transactions. However, Ethereum's blockchain is more versatile, featuring a Turing-complete programming language that allows developers to build a wide range of applications and execute complex smart contracts. be easily altered. This ensures transparency and security of transactions on the Bitcoin network.

ETHEREUM OBJETIVE

The objective of Vitalik Buterin in creating Ethereum was to democratize and decentralize the internet by providing a platform for building decentralized applications and smart contracts. He aimed to empower developers to create applications that are censorship-resistant, tamper-proof, and run without the need for intermediaries, ultimately fostering innovation and creating new possibilities for decentralized finance, governance, and more.
