Nigerian Court Delays Money Laundering Trial Against Binance

Nigerian Judge Postpones Money Laundering Trial Against Binance

Apparently, a Nigerian judge has postponed the trial against Binance and two executives until May 17 so that lawyers can review certain documents. The trial of Tigran Gambaryan, a Binance executive detained in Nigeria since February, has reportedly been postponed until May 17 following a further delay in the courts.

According to a Reuters report on May 2, lawyers representing Binance said they had not received the necessary documents to progress a money laundering case involving the company, Gambaryan, and regional director Nadeem Anjarwalla. The trial was expected to commence on May 2, but a judge postponed proceedings until May 17 to allow the legal team to review the documents.

New Developments in Binance and Executives' Legal Cases

The trial is separate from the tax evasion charges filed against Binance, Gambaryan, and Anjarwalla by the Economic and Financial Crimes Commission of Nigeria. All parties have pleaded not guilty in the money laundering case. Gambaryan's bail hearing is scheduled for May 17.

Anjarwalla and Gambaryan traveled to Nigeria in February to respond to allegations that Binance was manipulating the country's fiat currency, the naira. Authorities detained and charged both with tax evasion and money laundering, but apparently Anjarwalla escaped custody and fled to Kenya, where he holds citizenship in addition to British nationality. Interpol reported on April 30 that Anjarwalla's extradition proceedings were ongoing.

Many have criticized the Nigerian government's allegations as lacking foundation. The country's authorities detained both executives at the time when Binance announced its intention to cease all transactions in naira, raising questions about the timing of the arrests. Binance stated that Gambaryan had no "decision-making power" within the crypto firm.
